Background

My career started in El Salvador when I started pursuing my passion for robotics and automation. During my first semester in mechanical engineering at the University of El Salvador, I got the opportunity of studying abroad. That opportunity brought me to Canada where I studied Intrumentation and Control engineering. I worked in automation for about 3 years creating and programming electro-mechanical systems for the food industry with the goal of automating their production lines in multiple factories around the globe.

Later on, I decided to further my career and specialize in Software. During that time I started to work independently as a freelancer helping people build simple applications and websites while finishing my studies. This led me to a position as a Sofware Developer at the Center of Applied Research and Innovation where I've been working developing cool projects that aim to solve everyday problems and develop new technologies.
